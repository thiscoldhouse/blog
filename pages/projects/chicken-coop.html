The Chicken Coop: Theodora's Palace

Theodora is one of the chickens. Well, she's not *just* one of the chickens, she's the most important chicken. She's Theodora, empress of hens. Here she is, skeptically lording over all who enter.

This is a two part project. The first part involves converting an old milk shed into a chicken coop. The second attempts to automate watering the chickens through winter, without electricity or human intervention.

Part 1: Conversion

This old milk shed had a big milk fridge in it, and the walls were particle board. The old compressor was pretty cool, actually:

I considered seeing if it would run, but I had visions of explaining to an insurance agent why I need a new barn and decided against it.

So the old milk fridge, unfortunately, was a nasty, stainless steel contraption that had been assembled in place before the shed was built. It had to be cut to pieces to be removed. So I take out the sawzall, and immediately learn that this building has a wasp infestation, and they don't take kindly to the racket of sawing up this old fridge. After being stung twice, I suited up as if it was -20F outside (in July), and got back to work.

But after many sessions, we have a disassembled fridge! And also a random pink children's bike that was in the milk shed!

Part 2: Overcomplicating it!

Having chickens is cool, but in the winter, their water freezes. Normal people would just break the ice every morning, but I am not a normal person. Instead of spending 1 minute on this task every winter morning, I spent several days connecting my chicken coop to my spring well, so that there would be a continual flow of water from underground.

First, we dig a trench and run the black pipe!


Then, we build retaining walls! That requires moving huge rocks!

For the inside, I put up an old sink we had lying around. We'll put a 5 gallon bucket inside the sink basin, the water overflows from the bucket into the drain.
